Inside the Room

What Happens When You Show Up

Every session has a theme. Every person gets to speak. No one is required to perform, overshare, or have it figured out.

The room is structured — so it's not just venting — but warm enough that you'll feel the difference within the first 10 minutes.

1
0–10 min

Check-In

One word or one sentence. Where are you right now? That's it. No context required.

2
10–45 min

Facilitated Discussion

One theme. Real conversation. The facilitator guides without dominating. Everyone has room to contribute.

3
45–55 min

Reflection + Grounding

A short grounding practice to help you actually leave different than you arrived.

4
55–60 min

Close

Next week's theme. What you're taking with you. Done by the hour, every time.

Questions

Before You Show Up

Do I have to share?

No. You can listen as long as you need to. Most people are quiet for the first session and surprised by how much they want to talk by the second.

Is this therapy?

No. The 7 O'Clock Hour is a peer support group facilitated by a licensed therapist. It is not group therapy, does not constitute mental health treatment, and is not subject to clinical licensing requirements. Participants from any state are welcome.

What if I miss a week?

Come back when you can. The room runs every Wednesday. There's no catching up required — each session is self-contained.

How many people are in the room?

Between 8 and 12 people. Enough for real diversity of perspective. Small enough that nobody disappears.

What does "facilitated" mean?

It means Chude guides the conversation — introduces the theme, creates space for everyone to contribute, and keeps the room on track. It's structured enough to feel purposeful, but flexible enough to follow what's actually in the room.

Will my name or what I say be shared anywhere?

No. What happens in the room stays in the room. Participants are asked to honor confidentiality as a condition of joining.

Is it really free?

Yes. The 7 O'Clock Hour is free, every week, with no strings. Deeper paid options exist for those who want a smaller or more intensive experience — but this room is free because it should be.
